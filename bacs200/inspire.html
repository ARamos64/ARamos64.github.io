Piotr Szyhalski

He is a Polish-born, U.S based mutlimedia artist currently working as a professor at MCAD or Minneapolis College of Arts. His medium ranges from painting, photography, drawing, installations, or even sound. He explores historical phenomena and the relationships between the individual and society.

Born in Poland in 1967 during the Solidarity movement, he was inspired to create art that has the power to provoke discussions around complex social or political issues, ranging from war to economic disparity and the omnipotence of social media. One of his biggest influences was from a book called "Front Line Poster" which was comprised of art printed by soldiers deep in the trenches of war. After he moved to the United States in 1990, he studied poster design and drawing which brings us to his more famous series.

He is currently famous for his series of COVID-19:Labor Camp Reports which are drawings in black-and-white ink. These drawings are political statements of COVID reports, the response to the pain of the pandemic, various political issues and even covers sensitive topics such as police brutailty.
